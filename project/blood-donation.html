Frequently Asked Questions

Who can donate blood?

Most people can donate blood if they are in good health, weigh at least 50 kg, and are between 18-65 years old. There are some medical conditions and medications that may affect your eligibility.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made more frequently, up to 24 times a year.

How long does the donation process take?

The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes. However, you should plan to spend about an hour for the entire process, which includes registration, a health screening, and refreshments after donation.

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is safe. New, sterile, disposable equipment is used for each donor, so there's no risk of contracting a bloodborne infection by donating blood.

What should I do before donating blood?

  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Eat a healthy meal before your donation
  • Drink plenty of water or other non-alcoholic fluids
  • Bring your ID, a list of medications you're taking, and any required forms
